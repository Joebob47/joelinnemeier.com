Centro Histórico

El Centro Histórico is the Historic Center of Mexico City. It's an area where you can envelop yourself in Mexico City's history (and current governmental structure). The scale and magnificence of architecture, culture, art, and history will leave you in awe.

Activities

Mexico City Metropolitan Cathedral

This is the church to see in Mexico City. It is the most famous and most ornate. It's mesmerizing upon entering and seeing the scale and detail within. You'll notice in this historic center of Mexico City that many buildings have a lean to them, due to the fact that they are slowly sinking on the lake bed upon which Mexico City is built. To see the most extreme example of this lean, visit the nearby Santa Veracruz church.

Palacio de las Bellas Artes

A must see in Mexico City. El Palacio de las Bellas Artes houses excellent rotating art exhibits and permanent murals of fantastic importance. Many wonderful Diego Rivera murals are housed here (as well as throughout Mexico City). The buiding itself is gorgeous with ornate historical design and architecture. Check it out when you're in the area!

Food

Dulcería de Celaya

La Dulcería de Celaya is an iconic Mexico City candy shop. Nearly 150 years old, it is museum-like on the inside. A lot of interesting candy can be found here, and was notoriously a favorite of the famous Mexican artist, Frida Kahlo. Her favorite candy (and mine as well) are limones rellenos de coco (candied lemon rinds filled with cocount). Click here to go to their website and see their location. Be sure to stop here if you're near!

Tacuba 69

Tacuba 69 is a fantastic place to get tacos al pastor . Tacos al pastor is a specially-marinated pork on a spit, sliced to order and topped with grilled pineapple, all within a corn tortilla taco. Their reviews are fantastic and the atmosphere is great.

Things to Avoid

The zócalo metro station. Centro Histórico is a huge tourist attraction. Hoards of tourists attract scammers and thieves. A common scam is on the metro, where a group of four or five people will begin shoving people to exit and another is standing at the exit door. Amidst the confusion, some will be rifling smoothly through your pockets. Remember to be safe!
