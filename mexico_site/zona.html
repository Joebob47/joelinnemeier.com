Zona Rosa

Zona Rosa is the unofficial city center of Mexico City. This area is home to the largest office buildings and skyscrapers, as well as the famous Ángel de Independencia (pictured on the home page of this site). Zona Rosa is very lively; rhythmically pounding through the night to the music of the numerous nightclubs, and bustling with traffic and business people rushing to work during the day. Zona Rosa also has a thriving and open LGBTQ community, and is known as a safe space for the community's members.

Activities

Night Clubs

No need to mention where they are located. Just go to the neighborhood at night, and the lights and music will guide you. This area is packed with mainly LGBTQ nightclubs, but not exclusively. Come here for a fun night of drinks and dancing.

Pequeño Seúl (Koreatown)

A small korean enclave within Mexico City. A place worth checking out with great Korean food and a culture unique to the area. Also, make sure to check out Chinatown, near the Centro Histórico neighborhood!

Food

El Tizoncito

El Tizoncito serves up great tacos al pastor at an affordable price. It's a simple spot with delicious flavor. I frequented this place pretty often. Try the gringos , or tacos al pastor with melted cheese. They have a great refried bean salsa as well. Click here to find them and see the reviews.

Beatricita

Keeping the same recipes for over 110 years, Beatricita is a great location for all sorts of Mexican food classics ranging from tacos to mole and pozole. This restaurant is a must-visit in the area. A great article that dives into the history of the restaurant can be found here.
