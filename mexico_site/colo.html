Colonia Doctores

Colonia Doctores is sort of a central neighborhood in Mexico City. It is right on the border of the more trendy neighborhoods of Roma and Condesa, but packs in more exciting features. Where Roma and Condesa have long been known known for their quaint shopping centers, upscale restaurants, and hip culture, Colonia Doctores is an upcoming area with much to offer.

Activities

Arena México

This is a main attraction of Mexico City. Put this on your list of things you must do while here. Come watch the professional Luchadores fight with their own brand of professional wrestling. This is a real blast!

Monumento a la Revolución

This area holds many varying public activities. The monument itself is a beautiful piece of architecture, and within it a museum and an elevator to the top, which provides a fantastic view of the city!

Food

Ni Un Paso Sin Huarache

Huaraches are fried tortilla pockets, often times filled with refried beans, then topped with various meats, vegetables, and salsas. They're a great thing to try if you haven't had them before. Ni Un Paso Sin Huarache is one of my favorite restaurants in the area (get the huaraches) because of the taste and affordability. Click here to go to their website and see their location. Be sure to stop here if you're near!

El 123 Mini

El 123 Mini was my absolute favorite place to get a latte in Mexico City. The staff is wonderful, and the coffee was affordable and delicious. But, don't be fooled thinking it's just a coffee spot. My favorite breakfast I ate in Mexico City was here: Chilaquiles with green salsa and bourbon chicken! Click here to go to their website and get the location.

Things to Avoid

Window washers and traffic. If you're driving in the area, it's very common for window washers to come to your car without asking. They begin washing your window and demand payment. If you don't plan on paying them, it's best to sternly wave them off before they start. The traffic is also very heavy here, so use caution when driving in general.
