Chapultepec

This area is named for Bosque Chapultepec. The park itself is astonishing. Commonly referred to as one of Mexico City's "lungs", it is one of the largest city parks in the Western Hemisphere. It's big enough to get lost, so use the maps placed throughout the park as a reference. Inside the park are many places to relax in the grass, museums, funhouses, and even a zoo! This is a definite highlight in Mexico City, and a must-see for visitors looking for a break from the bustling city life.

Park Tips!

  • Bring a beach blanket! You'll see lots of people laying out in the open fields. This park is one of the best places to get respite from the busy city!
  • Many fun things in the park are free:
    • general entrance
    • the zoo
    • multiple museums
    • gardens
  • Getting here is easy. There is a stop named after Chapultepec on the Metro line 1 (see map on home page). If you don't want to use the metro, you can also check the metrobus schedule!

Activities

Chapultepec Zoo

The Chapultepec Zoo is definitely a surprise in terms of size and variety of animals. A great activitiy within the park, especially considering that there's no cost!

Chapultepec Castle

An awesome 18th century castle, housing the excellent Museo Nacional de Historia (National History Museum). It sits on top of the hill in the park, offering fantastic views of Mexico City, great art, beautiful gardens, and much more. Entrance fee: roughly $7.00 U.S.

Useful Spanish Phrases!

Do you have something without fish?
¿Hay algo sin pesca?
(Eye algo seen pehscah)
Do you have something that isn't very spicy?
¿Tiene algo que no pica mucho?
(teeyinay algo kay no peekah moochoh?)
Where is an exit?
¿Dónde está una salida?
(dohnday estah oonah saleeda?)
Where is a map?
¿Dónde hay un mapa?
(dohnday eye oon mahpah)

Food

El Pescadito Zona Rosa

El Pescadito Zona Rosa has great fish tacos of many different varieties. One of my favorite taco places in the city, it's located near the Zona Rosa entrance to Bosque Chapultepec. Be careful though, some of the salsas are very spicy, so ask "cuanto pica" (how spicy is it?). Click here to go to their website and see their location.

Food Trucks

There are various food trucks scattered throughout the park that offer basic Mexican food varieties. None really stood out, but it's a good option if you don't want to travel outside of the park for a meal!

Things to Avoid

Los payasos (clowns). This is only a warning if you fear being the center of attention. Clowns are scattered throughout the park, drawing crowds and playing various games with their viewers. The reason this is a potential warning for tourists is because they often choose tourists as their targets. I stopped to watch the clowns twice in the park, and each time I was chosen to be part of the show!
