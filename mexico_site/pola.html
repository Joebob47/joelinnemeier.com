Polanco

Polanco is the ritziest neighborhood in Mexico City. It's a mixture of upscale restaurants, fashionable boutique shopping, and pricier art markets. It's a great place to stop, eat, drink, and enjoy a calm walk amidst some excellent local parks and gardens.

Useful Spanish Phrases!

Do you have a smaller size?
¿Tiene un tama ño mas peque ño?
(Teeyinay ehsteh in oon tahmahnyo mahs peckenyo)
Do you have a bigger size?
¿Tiene un tamaño mas grande?
(Teeyinay ehsteh in oon tahmahnyo mahs grahnday)
I'm looking for...
Estoy buscando...
(Ehstoy buscahndoe)
Do you have this in a different color?
¿Tiene este en un color diferente?
(Teeyinay ehsteh in oon kolore differentay)

Activities

Museo Soumaya

Aside from the exquisite architecture of the building itself, this museum holds over 66,000 pieces of fantastic fine art. It is packed with art from different eras and genres. As with practically every museum in the city, it also holds work from Diego Rivera!

Antara Fashion Hall

An upscale outdoor shopping mall with big name brand stores and great restaurants. It's a wonderful place to walk around or do some shopping.

Food

Comedor Jacinta

Comedor Jacinta is a relatively cheap restaurant for its quality. Their chef is famous and the dishes served are traditional Mexican ones with a new wave twist. I recommend their lengua , or beef tongue. They also have great cocktails. If you haven't had mezcal before, this is a great time to try. Click here to go to their website and see their location. Be sure to stop here if you're near!

Pujol

Pujol is a world-famous restaurant with a world-famous chef. This restaurant has been ranked by many as one of the top 50 restaurants in North America. It is expensive, so be prepared to pay a high price for the experience! I never ate here myself, but have heard great things. Click here to go to their official website and get the location.
