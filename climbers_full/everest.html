Mt. Everest: The First Ascent

Sir Edmund Hillary

    In 1953, trudging up the mountain with a crew of 20 Sherpas, 350 porters, and a large amount of supplies a British military expedition set off to be the first ever to reach the peak of the world's tallest mountain, Mt. Everest.
    Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay would be the first in history to do so. Hillary was an experienced climber in incredible shape, but most likely not as experienced as Norgay, who had been part of six previous attempts to reach the summit, one of which reached 28,210 feet.

    Reaching the summit with a Sherpa was important for Hillary, who stated he wanted to "... include a Sherpa as a way of recognizing their invaluable contribution to the success of these expeditions."
    For years, the both decided not to say who technically reached the summit first. Years later in his autbiography, Tenzing admitted he was second behind Hillary. He was quoted later, playfully exclaiming, "If it is a shame to be the second man on Mount Everest, then I will have to live with this shame"
