K2: The First Ascent

Lino Lacadelli

    In 1954, One year after Everest's first recorded ascent, Lino Lacadelli and Achille Campagnoni ascended what some consider to be the most 'technically challenging' mountain in the world: K2. This was an enormous feat. The story of this expedition, however is fraught with controversy.
    While at a base camp near the summit, Lacadelli asked two climbers on his team to descend back down and grab oxygen masks for the pair. Two climbers agreed to the task, and descended as a favor. Some time after, Lacadelli and Campagnoni moved camp.

    When the climbers re-ascended to return the oxygen masks, they couldn't find Lacadelli and Campagnoni. They stayed out overnight in -50℃ weather. Ths resulted in one Hunza porters losing his fingers and toes from frostbite, among other injuries.
    It has been alleged that Lacadelli did this to own the glory, solely, of reaching the summit. This claim is vehemently denied by Campagnoni and Lacadelli, who claimed they moved because their camp was in a dangerous area. Shots have been fired in all directions, and the controversy lasts to this day. Regardless of what happened, the pair reached the summit on July 31st, 1954. Making their mark in history forever.
