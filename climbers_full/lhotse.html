Mt. Lhotse: The First Ascent

Ernst Reiss

    The ascent of Lhotse has garnered a little less attention than that of the previous mountains. This is most likely due to its proximity to Everest, coupled with the fact that Everest is seen as the ultimate achievement for a climber.
    As a result of this, it is difficult to find information regarding the ascent. What can be stated is that Reiss and Luchsinger were part of a Swiss expedition, who both reached the summit on May 18th, 1956. It is also recorded that on the way to the top, Fritz Luchsinger came down with appendicitis and was hospitalized momentarily before the ascent.
