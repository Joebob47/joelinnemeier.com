Mt. Kangchenjunga: The First Ascent

Joe Brown

    Although many had tried before, it wasn't until 1955 that George Bund and Joe Brown made the first ascent of Mt. Kangchenjunga, or "the Five Sacred Treasuries of the Snow". Their team of climbers was led by Dawa Tensing, known as the "King of the Sheraps" for his extraordinary resume, filled with many stories of high mountain ascents.
    The expedition was meant to be a recconaissencee mission, but upon reaching a certain point, it was decided that ascent was possible. They took their oxygen tanks and headed up.

    Brown and Band were part of a second team, who reached the summit after the first could not. Brown, interestingly, was the first working-class climber to join an official British climbing expedition. Brown led the way to the summit with Band just five feet behind him.
    Sikimmese beliefs (Sikim, India is the location of Kangchenjunga's peak) are that Kangchenjunga is a deity. Thus, in accordance with these beliefs, at 2:45 PM Band and Brown stopped many yards short of the actual summit cone.

George Band
